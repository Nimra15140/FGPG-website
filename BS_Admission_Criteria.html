introduction to bs Programmes

BS four year programm is termed as undergurate programme comprising of eight Semesters. In first two years, certian per requistie courses of Literature, Math,computer etc., provide general knowledge and a foundation of varity of subject perior to focusing on specfic field of study. In the third year, courses that lead to specialization are introduced. On the completion of four years and fulfillment of the university requirements,a student is awarded undergraduate degree.

Credit hours for undergurate and graduate degrees

1.A credit hour means teaching / learning a theory course for one hour each week thoughout the semester.

2.One CR in lab. Or practical work/ project would requir lab contact of three CRs per week.

3.The Credit hours are denoted by two digit within a bracket with a hyphen/plus in between.

4.The first digit represents the theory part while the second digit (rigt side of digit) represent the prectical work. Thus 4(3+1)/4(3-1) means a total of 4 CR of which, 3 are of theory while one CR is for Lab. Practical work.

promotion criteria

A Student must obtain the minimum Cumulative Grade point Average (CGPA) of 2.0 aat the end of each semester for promotion to the next semester , otherwise University will drop her.

Reperating courses/improvement of cgpa

1.If a student gets 'F' grade, he/ she will be required to repeat the course or its recommended alternative, if any.However,'F' grade obtained earlier will also be recorded on the transcript.

2.Undergurate student may be allowed to repeat a course in which he/she has obtained grade below 'C' . In such case both the previous and new grades abtained will be recorded on the transcript however only the higher grade shall be calculated for CGPA. The institution may define maximum number grater then 6 of courses that a student may br allowed to repeat in an eight-semester degree programme.

3.A graduate sttudent(MS/M.Phil) with a 'C' grade can repeat the courses to improve the grade. Each institutionmay define the maximum number of courses grater then 3 that a student may repeat. In such a case, both the courses and the grade obtained will be recorded on the transcript, however only the better grade shall be calculated in the CGPA . In case of CGPA improvement it would be recorded with (imp) on the transcript. .

Attendence

1.Attendence in classes is mandatory especially in a semester system. Each institution may developa policy for a minimum Attendence grater then 75% in course to complete the requirements of the university.

2.The instructor may report a student's absences and the student may be placed on attendence probation by his/ her dean/HOD and it will be notified by the department.

3.A student may be dropped from the university for violating the terms of such probation.
